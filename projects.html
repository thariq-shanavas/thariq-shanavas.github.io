I am currently working on evaluating the applicability of neural networks to improve optical communication channels. I am specifically working on pre-distortion techniques and adaptive equalisers.

Research Internship, KEK: High Energy Accelerator Research Organisation, Japan - November 2017 to present

I was involved in implementing control signals on an FPGA for a two-dimensional self triggered analog front end Pixel detector ASIC chip. The peak hold mechanism, following the signal conditioning circuit to facilitate data readout was suitably timed with the trigger system. The data is digitized by an analog to digital converter and logged into the PC through the FPGA. I also worked on noise optimisation of the signal conditioning circuit in the same ASIC chip for future iterations. Report

Research Internship, University of Alberta - May 2017 to July 2017
Analytical Modelling of Photonic Nanostructures

Numerically simulated and analytically modelled the optical properties of titanium dioxide nanostructures using electromagnetic theory, under the guidance of Dr Karthik Shankar, Department of Electrical and computer Engineering, University of Alberta, Canada.
I have theoretically modelled the observed surface plasmon resonances of the TiN coated TiO2 nanotubes, using effective medium theories and the exact Gans theory. Calculations agreed very well with experimental data.

Decentralised coverage control of mobile robots - november 2016 to Feb 2017

I worked on the control and coordination of decentralised autonomous agents with Professor Srikant Sukumar, Systems and Control Engineering, IIT Bombay. The propsed controller is suitable for decentralised sensing and action, for example, cleaning up oil spills or sensing nuclear radiation. A Lyapunov-type proof has been proposed for the stability and convergence of the system. The numerical simulations are found to agree very well with the coverage objective.

Matsya, autonomous underwater vehicle - october 2015 to October 2016

Matsya is an AUV designed and developed by a 30 member team of undergraduates spanning various branches of engineering like Electrical, Mechanical, Software and Materials science, at IIT Bombay. The team competes annually at the international RoboSub competition conducted by the AUVSI Foundation, San Diego, California, where we came second internationally last year.
My major contributions to the team include the DC-DC boost converter for powering up the thrusters and the motor driver module which cost around 20 percent of the market alternative, while at the same time providing twice the capacity. I have implemented hot swapping of batteries, allowing drained batteries to be replaced without switching off the on-board computer and at the same time providing an additional layer of safety in case of primary battery failure.

Simulation of spiral RF inductors - April 2016 to June 2016

I worked under Prof. Dipankar Saha, IIT Bombay, on the simulation and analysis of spiral RF inductors in the micron scale, using MATLAB and COMSOL Multiphysics. The major goals included finding a correlation between the trace thickness, width, number of turns and inductance value.
The results of simulation and experiment were found to agree very well for the test cases. I managed to isolate the major cause of deviation from ideal behaviour for the studied models to the current leaking through the substrate into the ground plane. I also roughly estimated the frequency at which the parasitic capacitance becomes dominant. I also tried out slightly modified models, which were found to have better characteristics than the original ones.

Plasma Speakers - April 2016
