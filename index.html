About me

I graduated from the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, with a major in Electrical engineering and a minor in Physics. I am currently a graduate student in Physics at the University of Colorado, Boulder, USA. I work with Prof. Juliet Gopinath at the Optics and Photonics Research Group.

My primary expertise is in experimental and computational optics, in particular super-resolution microscopy and non-linear processes in integrated photonic devices. I have a fair amount of experience with control systems, FPGA programming and Linux/Unix system administration, and a limited amount of cleanroom experience.

When I'm not in the lab, I read fiction, build and fly drones, play chess and board games, volunteer, bake cookies, and take pictures of cute animals in Boulder, Colorado. Also, Relevant XKCD.

I think Physics is fun, and want Physics to be fun for everyone! I am privileged to have led a team of six sophomore students as the manager of the Maths and Physics Club at IIT Bombay in the term 2017-18, tending to a community of over 600 enthusiasts in the campus. Do check out the club website and some of the blogs I've written here.
